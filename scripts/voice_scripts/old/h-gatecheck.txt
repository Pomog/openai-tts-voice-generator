Next, I check the route with EVE Gatecheck.

Gatecheck is useful because it shows recent kill activity along your route and helps you identify possible gate camps.

In my example, I can see activity near the entrance to Ahbazon.

That does not automatically mean death.

For a cloaky, fast-aligning ship, this may still be manageable.

But after refreshing the page, I can see suicide gank activity in another system.

That changes the risk.

This is why you do not check the route once and forget it.

You check it before undocking.

You check it before moving expensive cargo.

And if the page changes, your decision should change too.

Gatecheck is not perfect.

It cannot predict the future.

But it gives you information.

And information is cheaper than replacing your ship.
