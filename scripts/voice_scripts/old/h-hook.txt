Hauling in EVE Online is not dangerous because the route is long.

It is dangerous because you move expensive stuff without checking the route first.

In this video, I will use a simple example: moving items from Misaba, a low-sec system, to Jita.

The goal is not to make hauling completely safe. In EVE, nothing is completely safe.

The goal is to reduce stupid risks: gate camps, suicide gankers, bad docking, bad undocking, and warping gate to gate without scouting.

Before I undock an expensive ship, I want to answer one question:

Is this route safe enough today?

Not yesterday.

Not usually.

Today.
