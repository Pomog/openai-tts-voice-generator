And here it is.

My first-ever real PvP fight in EVE Online.

The plan is simple: start the fight with overheated high-slot modules, apply damage quickly, and try to break one target before the situation becomes impossible.

I choose the Kikimora as the first target.

But immediately, there is a problem.

The Kikimora is too far away.

My Rupture is moving only about two hundred and fifty meters per second, and my current falloff range is around fourteen kilometers.

At this distance, I cannot apply damage properly.

Then the fight gets worse.

My capacitor is completely drained.

The armor repairers become difficult to run, the afterburner is useless, and I am losing control of the fight.

And because this is my first PvP experience, I make even more mistakes.

I do not apply my autocannons properly.

I completely forget about my drones.

And instead of managing the fight, I mostly just watch my Rupture die.

So yes — I lose.

The ship is destroyed.

Not a glorious victory, not even a close fight.

But this is my first real PvP lesson.

Now the only thing left is to get the pod back to station, reship, and think about what just happened.
