We usually think of a dataset as a table, a column, or simply a list of numbers.

But there is another way to see it.

To begin, let us consider one numerical variable: one column containing several observations.

We can treat this entire column as a single vector, where each observation becomes one coordinate.

A dataset with three observations becomes a vector in three-dimensional space.

More generally, a dataset with n observations becomes a vector in n-dimensional space.

This geometric point of view allows us to reinterpret familiar statistical concepts using vector geometry.

The mean becomes a projection.

The deviations form a separate vector.

Variance and standard deviation come from the length of that deviation vector.

And correlation becomes the cosine of the angle between two deviation vectors.

We will build these ideas step by step.
