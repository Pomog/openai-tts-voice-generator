Now we simplify the equation for radial motion.

Radial motion means the asteroid moves directly toward the planet or directly away from the planet.

In that case, the tiny displacement vector points in the r direction.

So d r vector becomes d r multiplied by r-hat.

Now look at the dot product.

The gravitational force contains r-hat.

The radial displacement also contains r-hat.

And r-hat dotted with r-hat equals one.

So the vector expression becomes a scalar expression.

The left side becomes minus G M m times the integral of d r divided by r squared.

On the right side, if the motion is along one line, v vector dotted with d v vector becomes v d v.

So the equation becomes:

minus G M m times the integral of d r over r squared equals m times the integral of v d v.
