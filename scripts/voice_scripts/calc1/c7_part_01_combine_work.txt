Now let us combine the two conclusions we obtained for work.

On one side, we have the work done by gravity.

Work done by gravity is the integral of the gravitational force vector dotted with the tiny displacement vector.

The gravitational force vector is minus G M m divided by r squared, multiplied by r-hat.

The minus sign means gravity points toward smaller r.

So the gravitational work expression is the integral of minus G M m divided by r squared, times r-hat, dotted with d r vector.

On the other side, we have the work and kinetic energy result.

Work is equal to mass times the integral of velocity vector dotted with the tiny change in velocity vector.

So we set these two work expressions equal.

The gravitational work expression equals m times the integral of v vector dotted with d v vector.
