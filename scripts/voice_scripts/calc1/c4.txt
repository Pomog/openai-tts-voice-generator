The gravitational field affects our asteroid by doing work on it.

Work is the energy transferred to or from an object by a force acting along a displacement, the dot product.

If the force points along the displacement, the object gains kinetic energy.

If the force is perpendicular to the motion, it does not change the speed, but change velocity.

Now imagine the asteroid moves along a path.

The force becomes stronger as the asteroid gets closer to the planet and has an inverse-square relation with distance.

So we cannot always use one force multiplied by one distance.

Instead, we divide the path into many tiny displacements.

For each tiny displacement, we calculate a tiny amount of work.

Then calculus adds all these tiny pieces together.

That is why work is written as the integral of force dotted with displacement along the path.

Now we connect this to velocity.

Velocity is displacement per unit time.

So a displacement can be written as velocity multiplied by a time step.

Instead of force dotted with displacement, we can write force dotted with velocity, integrated over time.
