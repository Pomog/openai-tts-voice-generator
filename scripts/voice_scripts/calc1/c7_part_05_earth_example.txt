Now let us evaluate this for Earth.

We use the gravitational constant:

six point six seven four three times ten to the minus eleven meter cubed per kilogram per second squared.

We use the Earth mass:

five point nine seven two two times ten to the twenty-four kilograms.

And we use the Earth equatorial radius:

six point three seven eight times ten to the six meters.

Substituting these values into the formula gives:

v f is approximately one point one one seven nine times ten to the four meters per second.

That is about eleven thousand one hundred eighty meters per second.

Or eleven point one eight kilometers per second.

So an object starting from rest very far from Earth would reach approximately this speed when falling to Earth’s surface, if we ignore all other forces except Earth’s gravity.

By energy conservation, this same value is also the escape velocity from Earth’s surface, ignoring atmosphere and Earth rotation.
