Now consider the special case.

The asteroid starts from rest very far away.

So r zero approaches infinity.

And v zero is zero.

The asteroid falls to the planet surface.

So the final center-to-center distance is approximately the planet radius.

We call this final distance R.

In this case, one over r zero approaches zero.

And v zero squared is also zero.

So the equation becomes:

G M m divided by R equals m v f squared divided by two.

Now the asteroid mass appears on both sides.

So the asteroid mass cancels.

After rearranging, we get:

v f equals the square root of two G M divided by R.

This gives the speed reached by an object falling from rest from very far away, under gravity alone.
