Now we apply limits.

The distance changes from r zero, the starting distance, to r f, the final distance.

The speed changes from v zero, the starting speed, to v f, the final speed.

So we write:

minus G M m times the integral from r zero to r f of d r over r squared equals m times the integral from v zero to v f of v d v.

Now we integrate both sides.

The integral of one over r squared gives minus one over r.

The integral of v d v gives one half v squared.

After applying the limits, we get:

G M m times open bracket, one over r f minus one over r zero, close bracket,

equals

m times open bracket, one half v f squared minus one half v zero squared, close bracket.

This equation connects gravitational work with the change in kinetic energy.
