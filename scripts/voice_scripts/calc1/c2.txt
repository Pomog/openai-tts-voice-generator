Newton’s second law connects force and motion.

In Newton’s original wording, “motion” means what we now call momentum.

Momentum depends on the amount of matter in a body, the speed at which the body is moving, and the direction in which it is moving.

In modern notation, momentum is a vector quantity, defined as mass multiplied by velocity.

This means momentum depends on mass, speed, and direction.

Because velocity has direction, momentum has direction too.

Newton’s second law says that force is the rate at which momentum changes with time.

In calculus language, force is the time derivative of momentum.
