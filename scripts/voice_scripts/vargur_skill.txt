When new players hear about Sansha Incursions, many of them think they need to start with a very expensive battleship or even a Marauder.

For example, here is a Vargur fit used as one of the advanced Incursion options.

This ship is powerful, but the requirements are very high.

For an Alpha clone, without attribute remapping, the minimum skills just to use this fit take around 362 days.

And this is only the basic requirement to sit in the ship and online the modules.

It does not mean the pilot has strong damage, strong capacitor skills, good application, or good support skills.

A stronger Vargur skill target, such as T Badge Spec 4, takes around 453 days for an Alpha clone without attribute remapping.

This already includes better weapon performance and important damage skills, but it is still not the full recommended training level.

The full recommended Vargur skill plan is much longer.

For an Alpha clone without attribute remapping, it can take around 1214 days.

That includes long-term support skills: capacitor, targeting, shield, navigation, drones, overheating, damage application, and many other skills needed to fly the ship properly.

So yes, the requirements are serious.

A Vargur is not a beginner ship.

It is an advanced Incursion goal.

But this is the most important part:

You do not need to start with a Vargur.

You do not need to wait one year before you can start moving toward Incursions.

And you do not need to spend billions of ISK before you understand the fleet requirements.

Joining faster is possible.

A new or returning pilot can start the Incursion path within one month, and sometimes even faster, if they follow the correct route.

The correct route is simple:

Choose the right Incursion community.

Use their approved entry fits.

Train only the required skills for the beginner path.

Ask for a fit check before buying the ship.

And start with the correct fleet format.

This is why I do not recommend rushing directly into an expensive Marauder.

The Marauder is the destination.

It is not the first step.
