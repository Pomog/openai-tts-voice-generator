Many pilots think that if they are cloaked, they are safe.

That is wrong.

A cloak is strong, but it is not permission to go AFK.

Mobile Observatories can send system-wide decloaking pings with a chance to decloak cloaked ships. CCP describes them as a counter to cloaking-based activity in low-sec and null-sec.

So if you are cloaked in dangerous space, stay active.

Watch local.

Watch D-scan.

Watch overview.

Be ready to move.

And if your ship aligns slowly, stay aligned to your next safe spot.

For me, if the ship aligns in more than about three seconds, I do not want to sit still and relaxed.

I want to be prepared.

Because when something goes wrong, you may only have a few seconds to react.
