Now I will show how I get the blueprints using CONCORD loyalty points.

Detailed instructions on how to earn CONCORD LP are in my other video about Sansha Incursions. I will leave the link under this video.

To use CONCORD loyalty points, you need to dock at a CONCORD station with an LP store.

There are many possible ways to convert CONCORD LP into ISK.

You can buy implants, modules, blueprints, and different faction items.

But for this video, I am interested in capital module blueprints.

The goal is not just direct LP store selling.

The goal is to convert CONCORD LP into ISK through high-sec manufacturing.

So here I open the CONCORD LP store and search for the capital module blueprints.

First, I take one CONCORD Capital Armor Repairer blueprint.

This blueprint has five runs.

Then I take one CONCORD Capital Shield Booster blueprint.

This blueprint also has five runs.

Together, this gives me ten capital modules to manufacture.

Five armor repairers and five shield boosters.

Each blueprint costs 200,000 CONCORD loyalty points and 200 million ISK.

So for both blueprints, I spend 400,000 CONCORD loyalty points and 400 million ISK.

But the blueprint cost is only the first part.

After that, I still need to buy expensive Tech II capital modules and advanced components for production.

And this is where working capital becomes important.

I do not have enough liquid ISK to manufacture much more than ten modules in one cycle.

For this batch, I expect that around five billion ISK of working capital is needed.

That includes blueprints, components, job costs, and market movement before the products are sold.

So instead of putting all my CONCORD LP into more manufacturing jobs, I will also buy some implants for direct selling in Jita.

This gives me part of the LP conversion immediately, while the capital modules go through the longer industry chain.
